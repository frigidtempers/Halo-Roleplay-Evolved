# Covenant Remnants & Banished Forces

## Sangheili

Also known as Elites.

### Overview

The Sangheili are a highly martial alien species formerly central to the Covenant military hierarchy. While many now serve within the Swords of Sanghelios alongside humanity, numerous splinter groups, mercenaries, pirates, and Banished warbands remain active throughout known space.

Sangheili are disciplined combatants known for aggressive close-quarters assaults, tactical flexibility, and strong personal honor traditions.

### Common Equipment

- Plasma Rifles
    
- Carbines
    
- Needlers
    
- Energy Swords
    
- Active Camouflage Systems
    

### Combat Behavior

- Prefers coordinated assaults
    
- Frequently advances aggressively once shields are weakened
    
- Veteran Sangheili may employ stealth technology or energy swords
    
- Strong leadership presence among mixed alien groups
    

### Traveller Notes

- Stronger and more resilient than baseline humans
    
- Frequently equipped with Energy Shields
    
- Often possesses high Melee and Gun Combat skills
    

---

## Jiralhanae

Also known as Brutes.

### Overview

The Jiralhanae are massive, aggressive alien combatants heavily associated with the Banished. Unlike Sangheili military doctrine, Jiralhanae forces often favor overwhelming violence, intimidation, and shock assaults.

Their equipment is frequently rugged, improvised, or modified for brutality rather than precision.

### Common Equipment

- Spikers
    
- Gravity Hammers
    
- Manglers
    
- Brute Shots
    
- Heavy armor plating
    

### Combat Behavior

- Aggressive frontal assaults
    
- Uses intimidation and physical dominance
    
- Frequently relies on heavy weapons
    
- Banished warbands may utilize improvised fortifications and scavenged technology
    

### Traveller Notes

- Extremely high STR
    
- Dangerous in melee combat
    
- Often lightly disciplined compared to Sangheili
    
- May ignore injuries that would incapacitate humans
    

---

## Kig-Yar

Also known as Jackals.

### Overview

The Kig-Yar are agile alien scouts, marksmen, pirates, and raiders. Many serve as mercenaries or skirmishers within Covenant remnants and Banished forces.

They are opportunistic combatants who prefer ranged engagements and ambush tactics over direct confrontation.

### Common Equipment

- Needlers
    
- Carbines
    
- Energy Pistols
    
- Energy Shields (wrist-mounted)
    

### Combat Behavior

- Utilizes cover extensively
    
- Prefers long-range engagements
    
- Retreats when overwhelmed
    
- Often deployed as scouts or snipers
    

### Traveller Notes

- High DEX
    
- Lower durability than Sangheili or Jiralhanae
    
- Frequently equipped with portable shield systems
    

---

## Unggoy

Also known as Grunts.

### Overview

The Unggoy are small alien infantry commonly deployed in large numbers. Though often underestimated, Unggoy can be dangerous when organized or led by stronger Covenant species.

Many Banished Unggoy display greater aggression and independence than those formerly serving the Covenant.

### Common Equipment

- Plasma Pistols
    
- Needlers
    
- Fuel Rod variants
    
- Improvised explosives
    

### Combat Behavior

- Relies on numerical superiority
    
- Morale often dependent on leadership presence
    
- May panic if commanders are killed
    
- Dangerous in confined environments
    

### Traveller Notes

- Weak individually
    
- Most effective in groups
    
- Low morale outside organized command structures
    

---

## Mgalekgolo

Also known as Hunters.

### Overview

Mgalekgolo are massive combat forms created when large numbers of Lekgolo organisms bond together inside specialized armor. Hunters are among the most physically dangerous Covenant-era combatants encountered by humanity.

Though slow-moving, they possess immense resilience and devastating heavy weaponry.

### Common Equipment

- Assault Cannons
    
- Massive bonded armor plating
    
- Heavy melee shields
    

### Combat Behavior

- Advances relentlessly
    
- Uses suppressive heavy weapon fire
    
- Extremely dangerous at close range
    
- Commonly operates in bonded pairs
    

### Traveller Notes

- Extremely high Armor and STR
    
- Resistant to conventional small arms
    
- Slow movement speed
    
- Functions more like a light vehicle than infantry
    

---

## Huragok

Also known as Engineers.

### Overview

Huragok are floating biological organisms originally created by the Forerunners to maintain and repair advanced technology. They are generally non-combatants but are highly valuable strategic assets.

Numerous factions attempt to capture or protect Huragok due to their unparalleled engineering capabilities.

### Common Equipment

- Maintenance tools
    
- Support drones
    
- Technical systems
    

### Combat Behavior

- Avoids direct conflict
    
- Attempts escape or concealment
    
- May support allied forces through repairs or system manipulation
    

### Traveller Notes

- Rarely participates directly in combat
    
- Extremely valuable to military and intelligence organizations
    
- Capable of interacting with unfamiliar technology

# Species Templates (Player-Compatible)

> [!info] REFEREE USE  
> The following templates are designed for use when creating alien NPCs, hostile entities, or potential non-human player characters. These templates are intentionally lightweight and are designed to sit on top of standard Traveller character creation.

---

## Sangheili

### Characteristic Modifiers

- STR +2
    
- END +1
    
- SOC -1
    

### Traits

- Honor-Bound: Sangheili culture places heavy emphasis on martial conduct and personal reputation.
    
- Energy Shield Access: Sangheili frequently utilize personal shielding technology unavailable to most humans.
    

### Suggested Skills

- Melee
    
- Gun Combat
    
- Leadership
    
- Recon
    

### Notes

Sangheili are physically superior to baseline humans in most combat situations and are particularly dangerous in melee combat.
